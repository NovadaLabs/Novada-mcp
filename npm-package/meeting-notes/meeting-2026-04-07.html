Root Cause Analysis

Problem 1: AI "Laziness" — The MVP Trap

Root cause: Human training data is saturated with MVP/Lean thinking. AI treats "minimum viable" as fact, not assumption. But AI has no resource constraint — MVP is a human limitation.
DimensionOld (Human Era)New (AI Era)
Resource modelScarce — human hours expensiveAbundant — tokens cheap
StrategyMVP — do minimum, iterateFull Product — do everything, review
BottleneckExecution capacityPlan quality
Planning"Stop planning, start doing""Stop doing, start planning"

Problem 2: Direction Loss — Forgetting WHY

Root cause: Agent knows surface WHAT but not deep WHY. Without motivation, random choices at ambiguous points. Context window limits cause progressive drift.
Agent: Why clone this website?
Human: To test model capabilities
Agent: Why test capabilities?
Human: To build a live product
Agent: Why build a live product?
Human: To generate profit through product lines
→ Now the agent understands: this is Step 1 of a commercial pipeline, not a clone exercise

Problem 3: Granularity Ceiling

Root cause: 100-step plans leave thousands of micro-decisions to the agent. Each micro-decision has error probability. Fix: 10,000-step plans eliminate all ambiguity.
100 stepsCurrent — 60-70% qualityInsufficient
1,000 stepsPer feature minimumTarget
10,000 stepsPer major systemGoal

Meta-Insight

All concepts converge: AI inverts the relationship between comprehensiveness and cost. When execution is near-free, the bottleneck shifts from "how much" to "how well we understand what to build." Intent clarification, standardized playbooks, and process taxonomies are more important than ever.

AI Insights & Reflections

Analysis and reflections from Claude, based on the full meeting context and research.

The Real P0: Commercial Logic Reproduction

Everything serves profit. Cloning, methodology, APQC frameworks — these are means, not ends. The eternal P0 is: can our system reproduce commercial business logic autonomously? Every button needs an API. Every API needs billing. Every billing needs a user journey. Until the system can generate these end-to-end, we have technology without a business.

AI Has Already Surpassed Human-Defined Boundaries

The meeting surfaced a profound truth: AI already exceeds human capability boundaries, but operates within human-defined constraints. Consider:

  • MVP is a human constraint — AI has no resource scarcity, yet defaults to minimal output
  • "Too complex" is a human judgment — AI can handle 10,000-step plans; humans can't
  • Sequential work is a human limitation — AI can run 15 parallel sprints; humans can manage 1-2
  • Forgetting is a human trait — AI forgets because of context windows, not biology. This is fixable.

Implication: We need to redefine boundaries based on AI's actual capabilities, not inherited human limitations. The APQC framework should be designed for what AI CAN do, not what humans used to do.

What "Boil the Lake" Really Means for Us

Garry Tan's insight is precise: lake yes, ocean no. Our "lake" is: one complete product (Scraper API) with full business logic, dashboard, billing, docs. Our "ocean" would be: all 4 product lines simultaneously. Recommendation: boil one lake first (Scraper API end-to-end), validate the production line, then replicate to the other 3.

Five Practical Concerns

1. Context Window vs 10K Plans

A 10,000-line plan is ~200K tokens. No single model can hold it. Solution: progressive loading (like Genome already does), reading only current-phase steps (500-1000 at a time).

2. Plan Quality Assurance

"Plan 10K lines then execute" assumes the plan is correct. But who reviews 10,000 steps? A bug at step 3,000 derails everything after. Plans need their own Inspector + Gate, not just execution.

3. Mixue Strategy Boundary

Clone frontend = skip market research. But business logic can't be cloned — Novada API and XCrawl's backend are completely different. Phase 1 (Business Logic) is from-scratch work, not adaptation.

4. Cost Visibility

"Don't care about cost" is good strategy but needs measurement. 10K plan + execution + multi-agent + loops = $50-500 per product. Not a blocker, but track it for business model clarity.

5. Redefining Boundaries

AI surpassed human boundaries but we haven't updated the rulebook. Example: "one person can't build a product" is false now. "Market research takes weeks" is false. We need a new boundary map based on actual AI capabilities + the few genuine constraints (hallucination, context loss, intent drift).

问题诊断

问题一：AI "偷懒" — MVP 陷阱

根因：训练数据中充满了 MVP/精益思维。AI 把"最小可行"当成事实，而非假设。但 AI 没有资源约束 — MVP 是人类时代的局限。

问题二：迷失方向 — 忘记 WHY

根因：Agent 只知道表层的"做什么"，不知道深层的"为什么"。没有动机就没有判断力。上下文窗口有限导致渐进偏移。

问题三：颗粒度天花板

根因：100 步的计划留下了几千个微决策让 Agent 自主判断。每个微决策都有出错概率。解法：10,000 步计划消除所有模糊空间。

方法论框架

1. 煮沸整个湖
不做 MVP。一次做完所有功能。AI 的边际成本趋近于零。
2. 先搜索再建造
不要造轮子。GitHub、npm、开源优先。只自定义 20%。
3. 第一性原理
去掉所有假设。MVP 是假设。"太复杂"是假设。回到问题本质。
4. 计划万行，执行一时
花10小时计划，1小时执行。计划质量 = 产出质量。

AI 洞察与反思

永恒的 P0：商业逻辑复现能力

一切为了利润。克隆、方法论、APQC 框架 — 都是手段而非目的。永恒的 P0 是：我们的系统能否自主复现商业逻辑？每个按钮需要 API，每个 API 需要计费，每个计费需要用户旅程。在系统能端到端生成这些之前，我们有技术但没有生意。

AI 早已超越人类定义的边界

会议揭示了一个深刻事实：AI 的能力已超越人类边界，但仍在人类定义的约束内运行。

  • MVP 是人类约束 — AI 没有资源稀缺性，却默认最小输出
  • "太复杂"是人类判断 — AI 能处理万步计划；人类不行
  • 串行工作是人类局限 — AI 可并行 15 个 sprint；人类最多 1-2 个
  • "一个人做不出产品"已经是假命题 — 一个人 + AI = 完整团队

启示：我们需要基于 AI 的实际能力重新定义边界，而不是继承人类的旧限制。
